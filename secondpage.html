There are some nice images below of Tasmania. They should be loaded by the time you read this first section.

Tasmania is the big island south of Victoria on the south-east coast of Australia. There are not many people living there. There are some of the most beautiful rainforests in the world with many walking tracks leading through them.

For visitors from outside of Australia, there are many services available to make a holiday in Tasmania a time to remember. This is the place to come if you like the outdoors, seeing strange and wonderful wildlife in their (and our) natural habitat.

The weather is usually perfect in one part of Tasmania on any given day which makes it easy to plan a number of outdoor activities over the course of a week and get the right weather for each one.

There are many wild animals. Most are nocturnal. The best time to look for animals are at sunset and sunrise when they are most active (and still easy to see).

This is a photograph of Gordon River. Gordon River is on the West Coast of Tasmania. There aren't many built up areas here and it is a good place to go to see the true rugged Tasmania. Photograph by Geoff Murray, June 1996.

This is the Picton River. It is located in Esperance, Tasmania. This is a very peaceful scene photographed by Reade Allingham. This photograph is a brilliant example of the wilderness found in Tasmania.

Mt. Geryon is one of the mountains near Cradle Mountain. There are many good walks around this area. This photo was taken by Andrew Stevens in 1996.

Snorkelling in the clear, blue, waters of Adventure Bay/Bruny Island. The coast of Tasmania is a great spot for snorkelers. The sea life is beautiful and the water is perfect. This photo was taken by Geoff Murray in 1996
